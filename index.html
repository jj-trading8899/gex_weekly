Week-End Notes

NVDA — The 180 thesis failed, but the framework held. The GEX pin at 180 was real — $36.7M of positive gamma. But the broader market selloff (tariff fears, macro risk-off) overwhelmed the dealer hedging mechanics. The gamma pull works in normal conditions; it doesn't survive a macro event that triggers correlated selling across the entire options chain. The new structure is lower: pin at 172.5 ($20.4M), with 170 ($9.0M) as the stepping stone. 180 has flipped to heavy negative gamma (-$5.9M on 04/02) — the old magnet is now a repellor. Next week's trade is 167 → 172.5, but size at 50% after the miss.

TSLA — Best positioned for next week. The -6% drop brought spot right to the 360 pin ($4.8M). Unlike NVDA where spot is below the pin, TSLA is sitting on it. Positive gamma at 360 means dealers are stabilizing — buying dips, selling rips. The 04/02 expiry opens a wider range (355–385 all showing positive gamma). This is now the highest conviction setup: accumulate at 360 for a move to 365–375 next week.

AAPL — Slow grind setup. The pullback to 247.5 put spot on decent support ($2.2M). The 252.5 pin ($4.7M on 04/02) is 2% above — achievable but not overnight. Near-term dates still show negative gamma at 250–255, so Monday/Tuesday will be capped. The resolution comes Wednesday–Thursday as 04/02 approaches. Patient accumulation at 245–247.5.

TSM — Step aside. The neg gamma thesis played out to the downside. The 340 support we were accumulating around broke decisively. Spot is now in a negative gamma field between 315–335 where moves get amplified. The 360 pin is 11% away — irrelevant for near-term trading. Wait for spot to stabilize and new positive gamma to build around current levels before re-entering. This is the one to sit out next week.
